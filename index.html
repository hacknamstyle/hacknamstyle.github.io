Events

We are organising two workshops on real-world security issues on 25/03 and 26/03. On the basis of tasks from CTFs (security competitions), we will elaborate on the attack techniques used by attackers to break into systems, either by exploiting vulnerabilities in a web site, reverse engineering an executable, or leveraging cryptographic flaws.

During these workshops, we will guide you through solving challenges of various topics, so definitely bring your laptop to "get your hands dirty". The first workshop will be at an introductory level, so anyone with basic CS knowledge can join. In the next workshop we will try to tackle slightly more difficult tasks, though quick learners or people with some background in security can still join.

Practical details:

  • Introductory-level workshop: Wednesday 25/03 from 14:00 to 16:00, at 200A 05.152 (Java)
  • Intermediate-level workshop: Thursday 26/03 from 16:00 to 18:00, at 200A 05.128 (Prolog)
